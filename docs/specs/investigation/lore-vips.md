# Feature Spec: VIPs, Factions & Influencers (The Rulebreakers)

## 1. The Core VIP Mechanic
VIPs and Faction members are designed to break the standard rules of the restaurant (Time Crimes, Size Lies, Dress Code). 
- **The Trap:** If the player relies purely on the Master Clock or Party Ticket and accuses a VIP of a standard rule violation, they trigger a disastrous event.
- **The Solution:** The player must visually recognize the VIP/Faction using the **VIP Tab** on the clipboard, and click their name/clue on the clipboard *before* accusing them of anything. This acknowledges their status, safely bypasses the rules, and forces the player to seat them.

## 2. In-Universe Factions (The Power Players)

### The Syndicate (The Culinary Mafia)
- **Entering the Game:** They never make reservations. They spawn as Walk-ins in groups of 4 when the restaurant is at its busiest.
- **Visual Clue:** All four avatars are wearing **heavy, matching pinstripe suits**.
- **The VIP Tab Entry:** *"Watch out for the Pinstripes tonight."*
- **Game Impact:** They demand immediate seating, bypassing the entire queue. 
  - *If Seated (Bypassing rules):* They leave a massive briefcase of cash on the table, instantly boosting nightly revenue.
  - *If Refused/Made to Wait:* They smash the front window. The player takes a permanent Morale and Rating penalty for the rest of the shift.

### The Culinary Inquisition (The Critics)
- **Entering the Game:** They book under fake, aggressively normal names (e.g., "John Smith") and act like standard guests. 
- **Visual Clue:** They always wear a bright **crimson ascot** tucked into their shirt.
- **The VIP Tab Entry:** *"The Inquisition is doing random inspections. Look for the Crimson Ascot."*
- **Game Impact:** If refused for a fake name or a minor infraction, they drop their disguise. *"Do you know who I am? This establishment is a disgrace!"* They instantly drop the restaurant's rating to 1-star. The player MUST identify the ascot and seat them flawlessly.

### Old Money Aristocrats
- **Entering the Game:** They book a legitimate table, but arrive exactly **2 in-game hours late**.
- **Visual Clue:** Carrying a genetically modified, **glowing neon poodle**. 
- **The VIP Tab Entry:** *"The Duchess is coming. She brings her own lighting."*
- **Game Impact:** If the player clicks the clock to accuse them of a Time Crime, the player is fired. The player must click the VIP Tab clue to acknowledge their status, forcing a scramble on the Floorplan to fit them in even though their original table is long gone.

## 3. The Celebrity Parodies (The Egos)

### President Manu Macaroon (Politician)
- **Entering the Game:** Always strictly on time, but brings an unlisted +2 (Security).
- **Visual Clue:** Navy blue tailored suit, holding a tiny espresso cup.
- **Game Impact:** If forced to wait in the queue too long, he organizes a "Strike," permanently doubling the patience drain rate of everyone behind him in line.

### Donny Tromp (Politician)
- **Entering the Game:** Books a table for 2, arrives with a party of 6. 
- **Visual Clue:** Unnaturally orange face and a bright red tie hanging past his knees.
- **Game Impact:** Forcing him into a cropped table results in loud complaints (Rating drop), but he pays triple the normal cash.

### Mr. Feast (Influencer)
- **Entering the Game:** Arrives purely as a Walk-in when the grid is completely full.
- **Visual Clue:** Neon cyan-and-pink hoodie, standing with a wide-open mouth.
- **Game Impact:** If the player chooses to manually crop or kick out an honest seated guest to make room for him, he drops a massive tip, but destroys the restaurant's "vibe," angering Old Money guests.

### Gordon Angry (Rival Chef)
- **Entering the Game:** Books under a fake name.
- **Visual Clue:** Deep forehead wrinkles, holding two slices of white bread.
- **Game Impact:** He strictly tests the **Daily Menu**. If the menu is "Vegan" and he hates it, seating him is a trap. He will storm out mid-meal screaming about raw food. The player must actively refuse him based on the Menu mismatch.
