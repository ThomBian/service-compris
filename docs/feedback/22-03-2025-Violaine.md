✅ 1. UI Layout & Visual Clarity
Minigrid Placement: The Minigrid is not visible enough and is placed too far away from the doors.

Party Ticket Disconnect: The Party Ticket is positioned too far from the party at the desk, making it difficult for the player to link the ticket to the actual people.

Maître D' Confusion: It is not visually clear that the Maître D' represents the player, leading to unnecessary clicking.

Your Solution: Keep the Maître D', but change how they look. Add an animation of people moving toward them, and make a speech bubble pop up from the Maître D' to indicate they are welcoming the guests.

✅ 2. Game State Feedback & Cues
Missing Results: It is not clear to the player what the results of their actions are (e.g., when they are winning, losing, or when people storm out).

Table Timers: The player wants visual cues when a seated party is about to leave (e.g., a 10-minute warning).

✅ 3. Player Interactions & Controls
Welcoming Guests: The player instinctively clicked directly on the party in the queue to welcome them to the desk.

Seating Action: It was not clear that the player had to click on the doors to make the person sit down.

✅ 4. Game Balance & Mechanics
The "Arrived" Checkbox: The player did not use the "Arrived" checkbox because no guests were trying to impersonate a reservation.

Your Solution: Make reservation impersonators an active part of the game so this mechanic is actually used.

What Worked Well: Floorplan seating, grid interaction, and the struggle to place people (or being forced to refuse them) were highly engaging and worked as intended.
